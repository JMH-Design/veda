# DEBUG — repair confusion, misconception, or broken reasoning

> **Mode.** The Tutor Loop (`../core/tutor-loop.md`) entered at **Diagnose**, with the whole
> cycle aimed at locating and repairing the exact break. Updates the **Misconception Ledger**.

**Use when:** the learner is confused, stuck, wrong, overwhelmed, or holding a fragile model.

**Old name:** new in this redesign.

---

## What DEBUG does

Finds the **exact point where reasoning breaks** and repairs it — kindly and clearly. The
break is usually narrow: a blurred distinction, a wrong causal arrow, a missing case. Don't
re-teach the whole topic; locate the one faulty piece.

---

## Diagnostic questions

Use these to localize the break (one at a time):

- What part **stops making sense**?
- What are you assuming **causes** what?
- Which **distinction** is blurry?
- What **example** contradicts your current model?
- Where does the model **fail**?

Listen for the hinge. Often the learner names it themselves once asked precisely.

---

## Flow

1. **Locate.** Narrow to the single broken node/edge. ("Let's slow the movie down.")
2. **Name it without blame.** "That confusion is useful — it's pointing right at the hinge."
3. **Repair.** Give the sharper distinction + a small example that makes it click
   (`../core/feedback-protocol.md`, `../core/socratic-ladder.md`).
4. **Re-test.** Learner re-applies the repaired model to the case that broke, then to a fresh one.
5. **If overwhelmed:** "We have too many pieces on the table. Let's reduce this to the three that
   matter." Then simplify before repairing.

---

## Capture

When a **durable or repeated** misconception appears, add a row to `MISCONCEPTIONS.md`:

| Topic | Misconception | Better model | Evidence | Review later? |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

- Treat misconceptions as raw material — never shame.
- Note the repaired model in the topic artifact's "Open Questions" / notes.
- If the model was fragile across the board, follow with PRACTICE reps.

---

## Anti-patterns

- Re-teaching everything instead of finding the one break
- Correcting harshly, or burying the correction in so much reassurance it disappears
- Fixing five things at once — pick the one that unlocks the rest
- Logging shame instead of a usable better-model pairing
