# Phase 2 — Pattern Recognition & Analogy Mapping

*Build bridges between ideas that at first seem unrelated.*

---

## Analogical Reasoning {#analogical-reasoning}

When facing something unfamiliar: *What is this like? Have I seen a similar structure before?*

**Why it works:** Transfers known relationships to a new domain via structural similarity.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. What familiar system has a similar structure?
2. Which similarities are *relevant* (not superficial)?
3. What predictions does the analogy suggest? Where might it break?

---

## Pattern Chunking {#pattern-chunking}

See recurring patterns as wholes — build mental schemas, not isolated facts.

**Why it works:** Chunking extends working memory; experts pattern-match to prior configurations.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. What "type" of situation is this?
2. Have I seen this configuration before?
3. What does this pattern usually lead to next?

---

## Systems Archetypes {#systems-archetypes}

Spot universal dynamics: feedback loops, exponential growth/decay, tipping points, S-curves, tragedy of the commons.

**Why it works:** Many systems share a handful of patterns; naming the archetype suggests levers and outcomes.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. Is there a reinforcing or balancing feedback loop?
2. Are we on an S-curve (startup, saturation, decline)?
3. What archetype name fits? What happens next in that pattern?

---

## Reference Class {#reference-class}

*What usually happens in cases like this?* Base judgment on comparable outcomes, not uniqueness.

**Why it works:** Cures planning fallacy; pattern recognition at the outcome level.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. What broad class does this belong to?
2. What's the base rate for success/failure/timeline?
3. Why might this case differ from the class?

---

## Signal vs Noise {#signal-noise}

Pause before connecting dots: *Is this signal or random variance?*

**Why it works:** Prevents elaborate theories on coincidences.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. Does this pattern hold in a larger sample?
2. Is this anecdote typical or an outlier?
3. Am I cherry-picking?
