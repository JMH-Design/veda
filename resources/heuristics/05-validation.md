# Phase 5 — Validation & Feedback Heuristics

*Understanding isn't real until it survives contact with reality. Treat every insight as a hypothesis that needs to earn its keep.*

*(Inferred from User Manual intro — source page incomplete.)*

---

## Hypothesis Testing {#hypothesis-testing}

State beliefs as testable hypotheses before treating them as knowledge.

**Why it works:** Separates sounding good from being true.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. What's my hypothesis in one sentence?
2. What would confirm it? What would falsify it?
3. What's the cheapest test I can run?

---

## Seek Feedback {#seek-feedback}

Deliberately expose thinking to reality: experiments, critics, data, practitioners.

**Why it works:** External views catch blind spots self-interrogation misses.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. Who has seen this problem before?
2. What feedback can I get this week?
3. Am I asking confirmatory or disconfirmatory questions?

---

## Evidence & Model Adjustment {#evidence-adjustment}

When evidence contradicts the model, update the model — stay humble, stay curious.

**Why it works:** Rigid models decay; adaptive ones compound.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. What new evidence appeared since we last looked?
2. Which part of our understanding needs revision?
3. Are we moving goalposts or genuinely updating?

---

## Prediction Tracking {#prediction-tracking}

Make predictions explicit; review outcomes against them later.

**Why it works:** Calibration improves when you score your forecasts.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. What do I predict will happen, by when?
2. What confidence level (low / medium / high)?
3. Schedule a review date — what would we learn either way?
