# Phase 1 — Foundations of Understanding

*Deep understanding starts with breaking things down to essence and tracing how pieces fit together.*

---

## First Principles {#first-principles}

Reduce the problem or idea to its most basic, indisputable elements and build up from there.

**Why it works:** Strips misleading assumptions; fresh insights come from what's fundamentally true.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. What do we absolutely know to be true here?
2. What are we assuming without evidence?
3. If we rebuilt from scratch, what would we keep?

---

## Decomposition {#decomposition}

Divide a complex system into smaller parts; understand each in isolation, then reassemble.

**Why it works:** Brains connect smaller pieces better than monoliths.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. What are the major subsystems or components?
2. Which part is most confusing on its own?
3. How do the parts interact?

---

## Key Drivers {#key-drivers}

Find the small subset of factors with outsized impact (Pareto: 20% → 80%).

**Why it works:** Prioritizes high-leverage points for prediction and intervention.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. What few ideas does everything else depend on?
2. If you could only track three variables, which three?
3. What's noise vs. needle-mover?

---

## Structural Mapping {#structural-mapping}

Sketch how parts fit together — concept map, cause → effect, category → subcategory.

**Why it works:** Makes abstract relationships concrete; invites analogy once structure is visible.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. Can you draw the key components and links?
2. What causes what?
3. Does this structure resemble something you've seen before?

---

## Levels of Abstraction {#levels-of-abstraction}

Zoom in and out: grand architecture ↔ nitty-gritty detail.

**Why it works:** Insight comes from connecting layers; prevents tunnel vision.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. **Zoom out:** What's the big picture and purpose?
2. **Zoom in:** What mechanism or detail matters most?
3. Does the detail fit the broader theory?
