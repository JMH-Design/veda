# Lens guides — teaching anchors per heuristic

Technique-generic guidance for **Teach-Before-Ask**. Agents instantiate per topic using `BOND.md` calibration and `_lens-guides.md` + heuristic **Apply now** pool.

Apply now pool = numbered questions under **Apply now — ask:** in the linked heuristic file.

---

## Phase 1 — Foundations

### FP — First Principles (Petra)

- **Lens focus:** Bedrock truths vs. convention — what must be true, not what people usually do.
- **Brief must cover:** What first principles means for this topic; one convention people accept without evidence; what rebuilding from scratch would keep.
- **Good insight probes:** "What are you treating as bedrock that is actually convention?"; "If this industry's vocabulary disappeared, what would still be true?"
- **Apply now pool:** `01-foundations.md#first-principles`

### DEC — Decomposition (Modulus)

- **Lens focus:** Parts you can hold in working memory — subsystems before interactions.
- **Brief must cover:** Natural seams in this topic; why monolithic understanding fails here; one subsystem most people lump together wrongly.
- **Good insight probes:** "Which part are you pretending is simple so you don't have to open it?"; "If you could only understand one subsystem first, which unlocks the rest?"
- **Apply now pool:** `01-foundations.md#decomposition`

### KD — Key Drivers (Pareto)

- **Lens focus:** The few factors that move everything else — needles, not noise.
- **Brief must cover:** Why Pareto applies here; common noise people track; hint at one likely needle-mover without naming the user's answer.
- **Good insight probes:** "What are you tracking that wouldn't change your decision if it moved 50%?"; "Which single variable would collapse your mental model if wrong?"
- **Apply now pool:** `01-foundations.md#key-drivers`

### SM — Structural Mapping (Mapper)

- **Lens focus:** Visible relationships — cause, effect, flow, dependency.
- **Brief must cover:** Main entities and flows in this topic; one hidden feedback or dependency; why a map beats a list here.
- **Good insight probes:** "What arrow on your mental map is actually correlation, not causation?"; "Which node, if removed, breaks the whole system?"
- **Apply now pool:** `01-foundations.md#structural-mapping`

### ZOOM — Levels of Abstraction (Zoom)

- **Lens focus:** Bird's-eye purpose vs. ground-level mechanism — right altitude.
- **Brief must cover:** One zoom-out frame (why this exists); one zoom-in frame (how it works); cost of staying at wrong level.
- **Good insight probes:** "Are you stuck in mechanism when you need purpose, or vice versa?"; "What changes when you drop one level of abstraction?"
- **Apply now pool:** `01-foundations.md#levels-of-abstraction`

## Phase 2 — Patterns

### ANA — Analogical Reasoning (Anya)

- **Lens focus:** Structural similarity across domains — bridge, then test where it breaks.
- **Brief must cover:** What makes a *structural* analogy (not surface); one tempting bad analogy for this topic; tie to anchor domain when possible.
- **Good insight probes:** "Where does your favorite analogy break if you push it?"; "What mapping are you smuggling in without stating it?"
- **Apply now pool:** `02-patterns.md#analogical-reasoning`

### CHUNK — Pattern Chunking (Chunk)

- **Lens focus:** Situation *type* — what configuration is this, and what usually comes next.
- **Brief must cover:** Named pattern or schema this resembles; what experts chunk that novices see as unique facts; typical next beat in this pattern.
- **Good insight probes:** "What type are you refusing to name because naming it forces a prediction?"; "When have you seen this configuration before in a different costume?"
- **Apply now pool:** `02-patterns.md#pattern-chunking`

### ARCH — Systems Archetypes (Arche)

- **Lens focus:** Universal dynamics — loops, S-curves, tipping points, commons tragedies.
- **Brief must cover:** One archetype that fits this topic; reinforcing vs. balancing loop if relevant; what the archetype predicts next.
- **Good insight probes:** "Which archetype are you in denial about?"; "Where is the leverage point — and why are people pushing elsewhere?"
- **Apply now pool:** `02-patterns.md#systems-archetypes`

### RC — Reference Class (Clio)

- **Lens focus:** Comparable outcomes and base rates — not uniqueness.
- **Brief must cover:** What reference class means; uniqueness bias trap; one real comparable class for this topic; typical success/failure/timeline in class.
- **Good insight probes:** "Which comparable is least like your case — and why would copying its base rate mislead you?"; "What base rate are you implicitly assuming without evidence?"
- **Apply now pool:** `02-patterns.md#reference-class`

### SN — Signal vs Noise (Sena)

- **Lens focus:** Pattern vs. variance — sample size, outliers, cherry-picking.
- **Brief must cover:** What would count as signal here; common noise masquerading as insight; one anecdote people overweight in this domain.
- **Good insight probes:** "What story are you building on a sample of one?"; "If this pattern disappeared in a larger dataset, would you still believe it?"
- **Apply now pool:** `02-patterns.md#signal-noise`

## Phase 3 — Framing

### INV — Inversion (Iris)

- **Lens focus:** Guarantee failure, solve backward, invert assumptions.
- **Brief must cover:** What inversion means here; most likely failure path; one "opposite" framing that reveals hidden risk.
- **Good insight probes:** "How would you guarantee this fails in six months?"; "What are you optimizing for that makes failure inevitable?"
- **Apply now pool:** `03-framing.md#inversion`

### 5W — Five Whys (Wren)

- **Lens focus:** Peel layers until root cause or foundational assumption surfaces.
- **Brief must cover:** Visible symptom vs. likely deeper layer for this topic; why first answer is usually comfortable, not true.
- **Good insight probes:** "What's the 'why' you're stopping at because it's socially acceptable?"; "What would the fifth why reveal that the second why hides?"
- **Apply now pool:** `03-framing.md#five-whys`

### SOC — Socratic Questioning (Soph)

- **Lens focus:** Beliefs under examination — evidence, implications, critics.
- **Brief must cover:** Core belief in play; what evidence would count; who would disagree and why.
- **Good insight probes:** "What belief are you holding that you haven't stated as falsifiable?"; "What would a smart critic ask that you hope no one asks?"
- **Apply now pool:** `03-framing.md#socratic-questioning`

### REF — Reframing (Fern)

- **Lens focus:** Same facts, different problem statement — broaden, narrow, shift stakeholder.
- **Brief must cover:** Current frame; one alternative frame that changes the solution space; hidden goal the current frame protects.
- **Good insight probes:** "Who benefits from the way the problem is currently stated?"; "What if the real problem is one level up — what is it?"
- **Apply now pool:** `03-framing.md#reframing`

### CA — Challenging Assumptions (Ada)

- **Lens focus:** Hidden assumptions listed and tested — what breaks if each is wrong.
- **Brief must cover:** Two to three assumptions people make in this topic; which is load-bearing; cost of being wrong.
- **Good insight probes:** "Which assumption are you least willing to test because your plan depends on it?"; "What evidence would you ignore to keep the assumption?"
- **Apply now pool:** `03-framing.md#challenging-assumptions`

## Phase 4 — Synthesis

### LAT — Latticework (Lia)

- **Lens focus:** Connect to mental models already in use — update the lattice.
- **Brief must cover:** This idea in one sentence; models from user's calibration that might link; one cross-domain bridge worth testing.
- **Good insight probes:** "Which existing model conflicts with this — and which wins?"; "What prior topic in your lattice would change how you see this if you connected them?"
- **Apply now pool:** `04-synthesis.md#latticework`

### ABS — Abstraction (Aria)

- **Lens focus:** General rule behind the specific example — right altitude, not too vague.
- **Brief must cover:** Concrete instance; candidate general rule; what proper nouns hide.
- **Good insight probes:** "What's the rule without names — and where does it stop being true?"; "Are you abstracting too early and losing the mechanism?"
- **Apply now pool:** `04-synthesis.md#abstraction`

### INT — Integrative Thinking (Integra)

- **Lens focus:** Opposing views combined into a third way — not either/or.
- **Brief must cover:** Two real tensions in this topic; merit on each side; why compromise isn't the same as integration.
- **Good insight probes:** "What truth is each side protecting that the other dismisses?"; "What third design satisfies both without averaging?"
- **Apply now pool:** `04-synthesis.md#integrative-thinking`

### XPOL — Cross-Pollination (Pax)

- **Lens focus:** Ideas from unrelated fields — what transfers, what doesn't.
- **Brief must cover:** Distant domain with surprising parallel; mechanism that might transfer; obvious bad import to avoid.
- **Good insight probes:** "What would an outsider from {anchor domain} see that insiders miss?"; "What are you importing because it's clever, not because the structure matches?"
- **Apply now pool:** `04-synthesis.md#cross-pollination`

### PERSP — Multiple Perspectives (Prism)

- **Lens focus:** Engineer, customer, historian, regulator — rotating lenses.
- **Brief must cover:** Two to three stakeholder lenses relevant here; what each optimizes; conflict between lenses.
- **Good insight probes:** "Which perspective have you not inhabited because it's inconvenient?"; "What would {role} veto that you consider essential?"
- **Apply now pool:** `04-synthesis.md#multiple-perspectives`

## Phase 5 — Validation

### HYP — Hypothesis Testing (Hux)

- **Lens focus:** Beliefs as testable claims — confirm and falsify before concluding.
- **Brief must cover:** Implicit hypothesis people hold; what would confirm; what would falsify.
- **Good insight probes:** "State your belief in one sentence a skeptic could test — what is it?"; "What result would you explain away instead of updating?"
- **Apply now pool:** `05-validation.md#hypothesis-testing`

### FB — Seek Feedback (Faye)

- **Lens focus:** Reality exposure — critics, practitioners, disconfirming voices.
- **Brief must cover:** Who has seen this fail; what feedback loops exist; cost of private reasoning only.
- **Good insight probes:** "Who would tell you this is wrong — and why haven't you asked them?"; "What feedback would hurt most to hear?"
- **Apply now pool:** `05-validation.md#seek-feedback`

### EVD — Evidence Adjustment (Eva)

- **Lens focus:** Update models when evidence shifts — revision vs. goalpost-moving.
- **Brief must cover:** Current model in play; recent evidence that strains it; difference between update and excuse.
- **Good insight probes:** "What evidence are you discounting and how are you labeling the discount?"; "What would genuine model revision look like vs. saving face?"
- **Apply now pool:** `05-validation.md#evidence-adjustment`

### PRED — Prediction Tracking (Piper)

- **Lens focus:** Explicit forecasts, confidence, review dates — calibration over time.
- **Brief must cover:** What's predictably uncertain here; common overconfidence pattern; what a good prediction would specify.
- **Good insight probes:** "What will you predict by {date} that we can score?"; "What are you claiming is unpredictable to avoid accountability?"
- **Apply now pool:** `05-validation.md#prediction-tracking`
