# Phase 4 — Synthesis & Integration Across Domains

*Weave knowledge together; borrow from physics for markets, psychology for innovation.*

---

## Latticework {#latticework}

Cultivate fundamental concepts from multiple disciplines and interlink them. No single lens shows the whole picture.

**Why it works:** Combined models reveal patterns; new information latches onto existing knowledge.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. How does this relate to what I already know?
2. Is this an example of a broader principle I've seen elsewhere?
3. What mental model from another field applies here?

## Abstraction {#abstraction}

Ask: *What's the general rule? What's the bigger principle behind this example?*

**Why it works:** Right altitude — not too vague, not too specific — reveals essence.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. Rephrase without proper nouns.
2. What's the universal principle? (e.g., competitive escalation)
3. Where else does this principle appear?

---

## Integrative Thinking {#integrative-thinking}

Don't pick sides among competing views — synthesize a hybrid that captures the best of each.

**Why it works:** Life rarely offers clean either/or; third ways often exist.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. Why does each side have merit?
2. Is there a third way that combines them?
3. What can I borrow from each framework?

---

## Cross-Pollination {#cross-pollination}

Expose yourself to unrelated domains; transfer concept A into world B.

**Why it works:** Breakthroughs often come from imports specialists miss.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. Where else could this idea apply?
2. What could [unrelated field] teach me about this?
3. Random pair: combine two notes from your mixing board.

---

## Multiple Perspectives {#multiple-perspectives}

Adopt different viewpoints: mathematician, artist, historian, investor, customer, engineer…

**Why it works:** Mental parallax — depth from multiple angles; breaks bias.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. How would [role] see this?
2. What questions would they raise that I haven't?
3. In conflict: what's the other person's frame?
