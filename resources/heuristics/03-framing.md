# Phase 3 — Framing & Questioning Heuristics

*The quality of the solution depends on the quality of the question.*

---

## Inversion {#inversion}

Flip the problem: instead of how to achieve X, ask how to *prevent* X — then avoid those paths. Or solve backward from the goal.

**Why it works:** Surfaces hidden failure modes; trims confirmation bias.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. What would almost guarantee failure here?
2. If the goal were already achieved, what would have been true?
3. What's the opposite of what we're assuming?

---

## Five Whys {#five-whys}

Ask "why?" repeatedly (roughly five times) to reach root cause.

**Why it works:** Peels layers beyond the first easy answer.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. Why is this happening? (repeat until foundational)
2. What's the assumption at the bottom?
3. Can we act on that root?

---

## Socratic Questioning {#socratic-questioning}

Systematically probe: What do I believe? Why? What's the evidence? What if the opposite were true? Implications?

**Why it works:** Transforms passive thinking into active interrogation; pushes toward first principles.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. What assumptions am I making?
2. What would a well-informed critic say?
3. Where is my answer fuzzy ("I think," "I'm not sure") → research target?

---

## Reframing {#reframing}

Change how the problem is phrased: broaden, narrow, change outcome, adopt another stakeholder's view.

**Why it works:** Initial frame boxes you in; new wording opens solution space.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. How else could we state this problem?
2. What if the stated goal isn't the real goal?
3. How would [stakeholder] frame this?

---

## Challenging Assumptions {#challenging-assumptions}

Identify hidden assumptions in any belief, argument, or plan. *Is this necessarily true?*

**Why it works:** Strengthens foundation or prevents collapse from cracked scaffolding.

**Apply now — ask:**

1. List built-in assumptions (timeline, resources, demand, behavior).
2. What evidence supports each?
3. What breaks if one assumption is wrong?
